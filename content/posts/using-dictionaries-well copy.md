Dictionaries are essential for Chữ Nôm study, but a lookup is rarely the end of the work. Many forms carry several possible readings, and some readings only make sense in a specific sentence or genre.

When a dictionary gives more than one possibility, compare the surrounding Vietnamese words, the meter or prose structure, and the historical source. A likely reading should fit the text, not just the character entry.

## Compare sources

If one lookup tool gives a reading, check whether another source agrees. Differences are normal. They often point to variant usage, regional habits, or uncertainty in the source material.

## Save the citation

Whenever you make a note, include the dictionary or archive page where you found the reading. Future readers, including your future self, need to know where an interpretation came from.
